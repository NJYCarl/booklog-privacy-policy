# 我爱读书 / BookLog 隐私政策

更新日期：2026-05-16

本隐私政策说明“我爱读书 / BookLog”如何处理与读书记录相关的数据。BookLog 的 1.0 版本定位为本地优先的阅读记录工具，核心功能不需要登录，也不依赖开发者运营的云端账号服务。

## 1. 我们处理的数据

BookLog 可能在你的设备上保存以下数据：

- 书籍信息：书名、作者、ISBN、出版社、出版年份、页数、简介、封面、分类、标签、书架、套装。
- 阅读记录：阅读状态、当前页、开始日期、完成日期、评分、备注、阅读会话、阅读目标、统计结果。
- 书摘和书评：你主动输入的摘录、想法、长评、创建和更新时间。
- 本机账户资料：邮箱、用户名、显示名、头像样式、登录状态、密码 salt 和哈希。BookLog 不保存明文密码。
- App 偏好：主题、语言、默认排序、目标设置、Widget 摘要等。

这些数据默认保存在你的设备本地，不会上传到 BookLog 开发者服务器。

## 2. 权限使用

BookLog 会在需要时请求以下系统权限：

- 相机：用于扫描 ISBN 条码和拍摄书籍封面。
- 照片图库读取：用于选择书籍封面图片。
- 照片图库写入：用于在你主动操作时保存阅读分享图片到系统相册。

拒绝这些权限后，你仍可手动录入书籍、编辑阅读记录、查看统计并导出数据。

## 3. 可选联网功能

BookLog 的核心记录功能可离线使用。以下功能需要联网，并且只在你主动使用时发生：

- 联网图书搜索：当你使用国家图书馆或 Open Library 搜索时，BookLog 会把你输入的关键词、书名、作者或 ISBN 发送给所选搜索源，以便返回书籍元数据和封面信息。
- iCloud 同步：当你在设置页手动上传或合并 iCloud 快照时，书库快照会写入你自己的 Apple ID iCloud 私有数据库。开发者不会直接读取你的 iCloud 私有数据库。

BookLog 1.0 发布版不要求使用开发者运营的远端账号后端。如果未来版本提供远端账号或云同步服务，本政策会在启用前更新。

## 4. Widget、分享与导出

- Widget：BookLog 会通过 App Group 在本机保存轻量阅读摘要，用于 Widget 展示年度目标、在读数量和当前阅读摘要。
- 分享：分享文案和图片只在你主动点击后生成，并通过系统分享面板或本地文件处理。
- 导出：JSON 和 Markdown 导出文件由你主动生成并自行保存或分享。

系统分享面板、照片 App、iCloud 和第三方 App 的后续处理受相应服务的隐私政策约束。

## 5. 不追踪与不投放广告

BookLog 1.0 不包含广告 SDK，不进行跨 App 或跨网站追踪，不出售个人数据，也不使用第三方分析 SDK。

## 6. 数据删除

你可以在 App 内删除本机账户。删除本机账户会移除此设备上的账户资料和密码凭据，并退出当前登录，但不会自动删除你的书库、阅读记录、统计、封面或导出文件。

如需删除本地阅读数据，你可以在 App 内删除具体书籍和记录，或卸载 App 来移除本机 App 数据。已经手动上传到 iCloud 的快照由你的 Apple ID 和 iCloud 控制，你可以通过 iCloud 设置和 App 内同步操作管理。

## 7. 儿童隐私

BookLog 不是面向儿童的服务。BookLog 1.0 不主动收集儿童个人信息。

## 8. 政策更新

如果 BookLog 增加远端账号、云同步、分析、订阅或其他会改变数据处理方式的功能，本隐私政策会随版本更新，并在提交 App Store 前同步调整。

## 9. 联系方式

隐私相关问题请通过 App Store 产品页提供的支持渠道联系开发者。

# Privacy Policy

Last updated: 2026-05-16

This Privacy Policy explains how BookLog handles reading-related data. BookLog 1.0 is a local-first reading log. Core features do not require sign-in and do not depend on a developer-operated cloud account service.

## 1. Data handled by BookLog

BookLog may store the following data on your device:

- Book information: title, author, ISBN, publisher, publication year, page count, summary, cover image, category, tags, shelves, and series.
- Reading records: reading status, current page, start date, finish date, rating, notes, reading sessions, goals, and statistics.
- Excerpts and reviews: excerpts, personal thoughts, long-form reviews, and timestamps that you enter.
- Local account profile: email, username, display name, avatar style, sign-in state, password salt, and password hash. BookLog does not store plain-text passwords.
- App preferences: theme, language, default sort, goals, and Widget summary data.

By default, this data stays on your device and is not uploaded to a BookLog developer server.

## 2. Permissions

BookLog may request the following permissions when needed:

- Camera: to scan ISBN barcodes and take book cover photos.
- Photo library read access: to choose book cover images.
- Photo library add access: to save reading share images to Photos when you explicitly choose to do so.

If you deny these permissions, you can still manually enter books, edit reading records, view statistics, and export data.

## 3. Optional network features

BookLog's core logging features work offline. The following features require network access and only run when you use them:

- Online book search: when you use National Library of China search or Open Library search, BookLog sends your entered keyword, title, author, or ISBN to the selected source to retrieve book metadata and cover information.
- iCloud sync: when you manually upload or merge an iCloud snapshot in Settings, your library snapshot is stored in your own Apple ID private iCloud database. The developer does not directly access your private iCloud database.

BookLog 1.0 does not require a developer-operated remote account backend. If a future version enables remote accounts or cloud sync services, this policy will be updated before release.

## 4. Widgets, sharing, and export

- Widget: BookLog stores a lightweight reading summary locally through App Group storage so the Widget can show annual progress, current reading count, and current reading summary.
- Sharing: share text and images are generated only when you explicitly choose to share.
- Export: JSON and Markdown export files are generated only when you request them.

System share sheets, Photos, iCloud, and third-party apps handle subsequent processing under their own privacy policies.

## 5. No tracking and no ads

BookLog 1.0 does not include advertising SDKs, does not track you across apps or websites, does not sell personal data, and does not use third-party analytics SDKs.

## 6. Data deletion

You can delete a local account inside the app. Deleting a local account removes the local profile and password credential from this device and signs you out, but it does not automatically delete your library, reading records, statistics, cover images, or export files.

To delete local reading data, delete individual books and records in the app or uninstall the app to remove local app data. iCloud snapshots that you manually upload are controlled by your Apple ID and iCloud settings, and can be managed through iCloud settings and BookLog's sync actions.

## 7. Children's privacy

BookLog is not directed to children. BookLog 1.0 does not knowingly collect personal information from children.

## 8. Policy updates

If BookLog adds remote accounts, cloud sync, analytics, subscriptions, or other features that change data handling, this Privacy Policy will be updated before the App Store release that enables those features.

## 9. Contact

For privacy questions, contact the developer through the support channel listed on the App Store product page.
